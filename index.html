Month 1: Foundation Building

The first month focuses on establishing a solid foundation and creating consistent study habits.

Goals:

  • Establish a daily study routine
  • Develop basic listening comprehension
  • Build confidence with simple reading materials
  • Begin regular journal writing
  • Practice pronunciation through reading aloud

Weekly Breakdown:

Weeks 1-2: Listening and Reading Focus
  • Listening: 2 dalilk4english.com podcast episodes weekly
  • Reading: 15 minutes daily on LingQ with beginner-level content
  • Writing: Simple daily journal (3-5 sentences)
  • Speaking: Read aloud podcast transcripts for 5 minutes daily
Weeks 3-4: Speaking and Writing Introduction
  • Listening: Continue with 2 podcast episodes weekly
  • Reading: Increase to 20 minutes daily on LingQ
  • Writing: Daily journal (5-7 sentences) + summarize one podcast episode
  • Speaking: Shadow podcast speakers for 10 minutes daily

Month 2: Building Vocabulary

The second month focuses on expanding your vocabulary and using new words in context.

Goals:

  • Expand vocabulary by 200+ new words
  • Improve listening comprehension of natural speech
  • Read longer texts with better understanding
  • Write more complex journal entries
  • Speak with fewer pauses on familiar topics

Weekly Breakdown:

Weeks 5-6: Content Exploration
  • Listening: 3 dalilk4english.com podcast episodes weekly
  • Reading: 20 minutes daily on LingQ with content matching your interests
  • Writing: Create vocabulary lists from your reading + daily journal
  • Speaking: Record yourself summarizing what you read for 5 minutes daily
Weeks 7-8: Active Usage
  • Listening: Mix podcast episodes with English songs/videos
  • Reading: 25 minutes daily on LingQ, focusing on saving new vocabulary
  • Writing: Write short paragraphs using your new vocabulary
  • Speaking: Practice pronunciation of difficult words + read your written paragraphs aloud

Month 3: Conversation Building

The third month focuses on developing conversational skills and natural expression.

Goals:

  • Understand natural conversations between native speakers
  • Read and comprehend dialogue-heavy content
  • Write dialogue and conversational exchanges
  • Speak with improved intonation and rhythm
  • Respond to questions with less hesitation

Weekly Breakdown:

Weeks 9-10: Dialogue Focus
  • Listening: Focus on conversation-based podcasts from dalilk4english.com
  • Reading: Dialogue-heavy content on LingQ for 25 minutes daily
  • Writing: Create your own dialogues based on podcast scenarios
  • Speaking: Role-play both sides of conversations from podcasts
Weeks 11-12: Response Practice
  • Listening: Listen to interview-style podcasts and pause to answer questions
  • Reading: Read Q&A style articles on topics of interest
  • Writing: Practice writing responses to common questions
  • Speaking: Record yourself answering questions from podcasts with minimal pauses

Month 4: Fluency Development

The fourth month focuses on building fluency and natural expression in all skill areas.

Goals:

  • Follow extended audio content with good comprehension
  • Read longer texts with increased speed
  • Write more complex and cohesive paragraphs
  • Speak for longer periods without major pauses
  • Express opinions and ideas more clearly

Weekly Breakdown:

Weeks 13-14: Extended Content
  • Listening: Listen to longer podcast episodes (20+ minutes)
  • Reading: Read longer articles on LingQ (1000+ words)
  • Writing: Write multi-paragraph responses to topics from your reading
  • Speaking: Practice speaking continuously for 2-3 minutes on familiar topics
Weeks 15-16: Opinion Expression
  • Listening: Focus on debate or discussion podcasts
  • Reading: Read opinion pieces and editorials
  • Writing: Write opinion essays on topics you've read about
  • Speaking: Record yourself expressing and supporting opinions on various topics

The fifth month focuses on expanding the range of content you can understand and produce.

Month 6: Integration and Real-World Application

The final month focuses on integrating all skills and applying them in real-world contexts.

Goals:

  • Understand authentic content without significant difficulty
  • Read native-level materials with good comprehension
  • Write clear, well-structured texts on various topics
  • Speak confidently in different situations
  • Use English effectively for practical purposes

Weekly Breakdown:

Weeks 21-22: Practical Application
  • Listening: Focus on practical content (instructions, explanations, discussions)
  • Reading: Read practical texts (guides, manuals, instructions)
  • Writing: Practice practical writing (emails, messages, summaries)
  • Speaking: Role-play practical scenarios (giving directions, explaining processes)
Weeks 23-24: Integration and Assessment
  • Listening: Challenge yourself with unedited native content
  • Reading: Read authentic materials at native speed
  • Writing: Create longer, cohesive texts integrating all you've learned
  • Speaking: Record extended talks on complex topics
  • Assessment: Evaluate your progress in all skill areas

Daily Study Schedule

Make the most of your 1-hour daily study time with this balanced schedule that covers all language skills:

10 min

Listening Practice

Podcasts from dalilk4english.com, songs, videos, or other audio content

Tips:
  • Listen actively, not passively
  • First time: focus on general understanding
  • Second time: focus on details and new words
20 min

Reading Practice

Content on LingQ, articles, stories, or other written materials

Tips:
  • Choose content slightly above your level
  • Look up only essential words
  • Save new vocabulary for review
15 min

Writing Practice

Journal entries, summaries, responses to content, or creative writing

Tips:
  • Write without translation
  • Use new vocabulary from your reading
  • Focus on expressing ideas clearly
15 min

Speaking Practice

Reading aloud, shadowing, recording yourself, or conversation practice

Tips:
  • Record yourself to track improvement
  • Practice even if you feel shy
  • Focus on fluency over perfection

Schedule Flexibility:

You can adjust this schedule based on your needs and preferences:

  • Morning Routine: Do all activities in one 1-hour session
  • Split Schedule: Divide activities throughout the day (e.g., listening in the morning, reading at lunch, etc.)
  • Focus Days: Emphasize different skills on different days while maintaining balance across the week

Tips for Success

Consistency is Key

Study every day, even if it's just for 30 minutes. Regular practice is more effective than occasional long sessions.

Active Learning

Don't just passively consume content. Interact with it by taking notes, answering questions, and creating your own examples.

Spaced Repetition

Review vocabulary and concepts at increasing intervals to strengthen your memory and retention.

Follow Your Interests

Choose topics you're passionate about. You'll learn more effectively when you're engaged with the content.

Speak From Day One

Don't wait until you feel "ready" to speak. Practice speaking from the beginning, even if you make mistakes.

Find a Community

Connect with other English learners or native speakers. Shared learning experiences can boost motivation and provide support.

Track Your Progress

Regularly assess your skills to see how far you've come. Celebrate your improvements, no matter how small.

Learn Before Sleep

Studying right before bedtime can improve memory consolidation as your brain processes information during sleep.

Embrace Mistakes

Don't fear errorsthey're a natural part of learning. Each mistake is an opportunity to improve.

Immerse Yourself

Surround yourself with English whenever possible. Change your phone language, watch shows in English, and think in English.
